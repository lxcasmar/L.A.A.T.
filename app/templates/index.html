What are Technical Indicators?

Simply put, a technical indicator is a mathematical calculation based on the price, volume, or open interest of a security (a stock). This is used to predict future price movements of the stock. Technical indicators can be divided into 4 main groups: trend, momentum, Volatility, and volume indicators. Here, you will see that our strategies combine multiple indicators. Note, however, that two indicators from the same category are never used together. Combining indicators from the same category could lead to wrong price interpretation, confusion, and wrong trade decisions. For future reference, note that a number preceeding an indicator name specifies a generic time frame. I.e. 100EMA represents the 100-day/minute/second EMA

Types of Indicators

  • Trend Indicators

    These indicators measure the direction and strength of an established trend in the market. The direction of price movement indicates trend strength by measuring whether buyers or sellers are willing and able to push the price beyond the previous high/low. Trend indicators currently in use at L.A.A.T.:

    • EMA - Exponential Moving Average

      In general, a moving average is a calculation that measures the average change in price during a specific time period. It is used to filter out the "noise" of short term fluctuations. Exponential moving averages place more importance on more recent prices

  • Momentum Indicators

    Momentum indicators measure how fast price changes over a specific time period. Note that momentum indicators do not reflect the direction of price change, only the speed of change. Momentum indicators currently in use at L.A.A.T.:

    • RSI - Relative Strength Index

      This measures the magnitude of price changes over a time period to determine if a stock is currently overbought or oversold.

    • MACD - Moving Average Convergence Divergence

  • Volatility Indicators

    Volatility indicators measure the amount of market activity in a security. When volatility is high, there is plenty of buying and selling. On the other hand, low volatility indicates relatively low trading activity. Volatility indicators currently in use at L.A.A.T:

    • Bbands - Bollinger Bands

  • Volume Indicators

    The last type of indicator measures how interested buyers and sellers are in a security by tracking things like buying and selling activity. Volume indicators currently in use at L.A.A.T:

    • OBV - On-balance Volume
